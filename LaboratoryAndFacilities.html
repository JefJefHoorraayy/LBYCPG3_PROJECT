Laboratory and Facilities

The Department of Chemical Engineering hosts several state-of-the-art equipments in its various laboratories, dedicated both to research work and to student education.


Distillation Equipment with Rectification

Used to perform distillation experiments, with or without reflux, using a sieved or packed column, under ambient pressure or under vacuum.

Location: Velasco Building 102-A

Multifunction Process Control Teaching System (Armfield PCT40)

Used to study wide range of process control techniques in a simple basic unit, including temperature measurement and flow control.

Location: Velasco Building 101

Instrumentation Room

Houses several equipment used for research in Chemical Engineering, such as the GC-ECD and the HPLC.

Location: STRC 215 and STRC 217

Thermogravimetric Analyzer (TGA-Q50)

Measures mass changes and rate of weight change under controlled temperature and atmosphere. Used for pyrolysis, gasification and combustion studies of various materials.

Location: STRC 215 and STRC 217

Biomaterials and Tissue Engineering Laboratory 1 Science & Technology Research Center – Room 107B 17.04 The Biomaterials and Tissue Engineering Laboratory 1 serves as the “wet” laboratory for the Biomaterials and Tissue Engineering group, where all decellularization experiments are conducted. It also serves as the venue for the preparation of the polymer solutions used during electrospinning and 3D bioprinting in the Biomaterials and Tissue Engineering Laboratory 2. Fume hood, Bath type sonicator, Distillation column, Peristaltic pumps, Decellularization chamber, Touch Screen Viscometer, Analytical Balance, Magnetic Stirrer w/ Hot Plate
Biomaterials and Tissue Engineering Laboratory 2 Science & Technology Research Center – Room 214 32.00 The Biomaterials and Tissue Engineering Laboratory 2 serves as the “dry” laboratory of the Biomaterials and Tissue Engineering group. The facility is primarily used to fabricate engineered tissue scaffolds from polymeric biomaterials. The laboratory currently houses the country’s most advanced rapid biofabrication machines, namely the eleectrospinner and 3D bioprinter. Multi Nozzle Electrospinning Machine, 3D Bioprinter, CATIA V6, Universal Biomaterials Testing Machine

Research topics include, but are not limited to, biofuels, carbon capture, carbon nanotubes production, biological nutrient removal, biogas production, fermentation, and essential oil extraction. Atomic Absorption Spectrometer (AAS), Rotary Evaporator, Gas Chromatograph (GC), Total Organic Carbon (TOC) Analyzer, Centrifuge, Shaking Incubator, Overhead Stirring Apparatus, Water Distillation Unit, Water Deionizer Unit, Solarimeter
Energy Laboratory Science & Technology Research Center – Room 107 46.14 The Energy Laboratory provides the venue for research on energy extraction from renewable and non-renewable resources by gasification, pyrolysis, and combustion, including clean coal technology research and the transesterification of different feedstock for the production of biodiesel. Thermal Gravimetric Analyzer (TGA), Differential Scanning Calorimeter (DSC), Furnace, Fume Hood

Jar Testing Apparatus, Fume Hood, Drying Oven, Sonicator, Temperature Regulated Water Bath, Shaking Incubator, Rotary drum mixer, Vortex Mixer, DO Meter, pH Meter

DProcess Control and Simulation Laboratory Velasco Hall – Room 101 67.48 Being an instructional laboratory, students are exposed to experiments in process control demonstrating the principles of transfer functions, electronic controllers (e.g. PID), controller tuning, and their typical industrial applications. In addition to bench-scale process apparatuses, specialized process modelling software are also available in this laboratory. Various Armfield® Multifunction Process Control Teaching Systems, OSK® Temperature Control Plant, Data Acquisition Systems from National Instruments, Taylor® Water Level Control Demonstration Unit, SUPERPRO DESIGNER (flow sheet simulation software), HYPER LINGO (optimization software), UniSim Design (software for process design and simulation)
Unit Operations Laboratory Velasco Hall – Room 102 & Miguel Hall – Ground Floor 107.25 (Velasco Hall)+36.00 (Miguel Hall) The Unit Operations Laboratory serves the needs of undergraduate students in chemical engineering. Typical experiments cover topics in fluid mechanics (such as pump characteristics and fluid agitation), heat transfer (such as radiation), mass transfer operations (such as diffusivities, binary distillation, gas absorption, humidification, drying, adsorption), mechanical separation (such as filtration, screening, size reduction), and other related topics like reaction engineering (such as plug flow or tubular reactor). In addition, routine sample preparation and separation, as part of preliminary procedures in research, are often performed here, such as size reduction, centrifugation, drying, elevated heating, sieving and weighing, and catalyst preparation. CSTR Plug Flow Reactor, Cooling Tower, Packed Column Gas Absorption Unit, Distillation Unit, Plate and Frame Filtration Unit, Leaf Filtration Unit, Ion Exchange Unit, Thermal Radiation Equipment, Tray Dryer, Muffle Furnace, Drying Oven, Fume Hood, Series-Parallel Centrifugal Pumps Set-up, Liquid-Solid Fluidization Apparatus, Unsteady State Liquid Flow Apparatus, Single-Effect Steam-Jacketed Evaporator, Single and Double Effect Evaporator System, Sieve Shaker, Centrifuge, Wiley Mill, Ball Mill and Jaw Crusher, Liquid Agitation and Mixing Unit
